What a Daily Work Log Should Capture

Whether you're a superintendent, foreman, or project manager, your daily log should answer these questions:

  1. What work was done today? — Tasks completed, areas worked, progress made
  2. Who was on site? — Headcount, subcontractors, visitors
  3. What were the conditions? — Weather, access, any disruptions
  4. Were there any safety issues? — Hazards observed, near-misses, incidents
  5. What materials and equipment were used? — Deliveries, equipment hours, consumables

A good daily log app makes capturing this information fast enough that it actually gets done every day — not just when someone remembers.

The 2-minute test: If your daily log takes more than 2 minutes to start, your field team won't use it consistently. BuildLog is designed around this constraint.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does BuildLog work without internet?

Yes. BuildLog saves everything locally on your device first. When you reconnect, it syncs automatically. You'll never lose a report because of bad signal.

Do I need to download anything?

No. BuildLog is a Progressive Web App (PWA) that runs in your browser. You can add it to your home screen for an app-like experience, but there's nothing to install from an app store.

Can I use voice to log?

Yes. Tap the record button, describe what happened, and BuildLog transcribes it into a report entry. Most field supervisors find this faster than typing.

What kind of reports can I export?

BuildLog generates professional PDF and CSV reports that include all report details, photos, timestamps, and GPS coordinates. These are suitable for clients, inspectors, and internal records.

Is my data secure?
