Tips for Effective Documentation

1. Document in Real-Time

Don't wait until the end of the day. Record information as it happens to ensure accuracy and completeness.

2. Be Specific

Use detailed, specific descriptions rather than vague statements. Include exact times, locations, and names when relevant.

3. Use Visual Evidence

Photos with GPS geotagging provide irrefutable evidence of site conditions, safety measures, and work completed.

4. Stay Consistent

Use the same format and structure every day to make reports easy to review, compare, and audit.

5. Review Before Finalizing

Always review your documentation against this checklist before finalizing to ensure nothing is missed.

Common Documentation Mistakes

  • Missing required OSHA elements
  • Vague or incomplete descriptions
  • Forgetting to document near-misses
  • Not including visual evidence
  • Inconsistent formatting
  • Delayed documentation leading to forgotten details
